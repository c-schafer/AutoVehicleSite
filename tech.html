Five Levels of Autonomous Driving

  • Some safety features, such as lane assist, are included in the vehicle. Adaptive cruise control may be included, as well as emergency braking.
  • Adaptive cruise control and other Level 1 features are included, but blind spot detection, automated parking, and sometimes traffic sign recognition are included as well.
  • Level 3 is what we usually consider "self-driving." The vehicle can accelerate, brake, steer, park, navigate traffic signs and signals, and more, using complex algorithms.
  • Once vehicles reach Level 4 automation, human drivers are rarely needed to take over. They can manage complex and difficult situations. Waymo Taxis fit into this category.
  • With full automation, a human driver is entirely unnecessary. Theoretically, a Level 5 vehicle can operate safely under any conditions, but no company has reached this stage yet.
